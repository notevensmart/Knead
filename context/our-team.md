# Our Team

## Siri Indukuri (She/Her)

Siri is passionate about promoting awareness of mental health and wellbeing across all age groups. She has worked in the field for the past 10 years with people of diverse ages and backgrounds and is a strong advocate of everyone having equal access to mental health care.

Siri has particularly worked with adolescents, teenagers and young adults and recognises the impact of the ever changing expectations of these age groups. She believes that these age groups have an increased proclivity for positive change and developing resilience factors. She vouches for adaptation of protective factors that can buffer the development of lifelong mental health disorders.

Siri moved from Melbourne (Australia) to Hyderabad last March to further her knowledge on social justice stance of mental health work, anti-caste and trauma work as well as neurodivergent affirmative care. She also believes that these learnings are long term and continues to educate herself in areas of mental health work that enrichen the therapeutic process, specifically in the Indian context.

## Vaishnavi Gattu (She/Her)

Vaishnavi is continuously working towards being a compassionate and an empathetic clinical social worker. She specialises in working with adolescents, teenagers and young adults. She is also a trained behavioural therapist who has extensive experience working with children who have special needs.

Additionally, Vaishnavi is also trained in parent/guardian, teacher and other care team sessions to increase and deepen support networks for her clients. She is currently working with school going adolescents and teenagers addressing a range of concerns including, but not limited to, anxiety and mood disorders, grief and trauma, neurodivergence, psychosocial developmental issues.

For her, all individuals need a safe space to explore their growth and resolve concerns. She believes that her work is a small change towards promoting social justice and diversity within the mental health space. She practices eclectic approaches, designed according to clients and their concern, therefore facilitating client centered therapy.
